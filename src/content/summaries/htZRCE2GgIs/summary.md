## Key Takeaways

The most effective AI productivity strategy is selecting tools based on their unique strengths rather than using one for everything. The key insights are:

*   **Match the tool to the task:** Each leading AI has a distinct "superpower"‚ÄîChatGPT's **obedience**, Gemini's **multimodality**, Claude's **draft quality**, Perplexity's **search speed**, and Notebook LM's **source-grounded accuracy**.

*   **Use a layered workflow:** Start a project with ChatGPT/Gemini for ideation and research, then use Claude for final polishing and code generation.

*   **Specialist vs. Generalist:** Use general chatbots (ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude) for reasoning and creation, and specialist tools (Perplexity, Notebook LM) for specific tasks like verification and fact-checking.

## Summary

Jeff Su explains that his productivity comes from using a small set of AI tools, each chosen for a specific, superior capability. He groups them into "Everyday AI" (general chatbots) and "Specialist AI" (task-optimized tools).

### Everyday AI: General-Purpose Chatbots

*   **ChatGPT's Superpower: Obedience.** It reliably follows lengthy, complex checklists without skipping steps, making it ideal for tasks where missing one instruction breaks the whole process (e.g., creating a detailed hiring rubric).

*   **Gemini's Superpower: Multimodality & Massive Context.** It natively processes video, audio, images, and text together within a 1-million-token context window. It's the best tool for synthesizing information from mixed media, like turning a meeting recording, slides, and whiteboard photo into a summary and follow-up email.

*   **Claude's Superpower: High-Quality First Drafts.** It excels at producing near-final output on the first try, particularly for **coding** (writing functional scripts) and **polishing copy** (matching tone and style from examples).

**Workflow Integration:** Use ChatGPT or Gemini at the *beginning* of work for brainstorming and research, then hand off the rough output to Claude for the *final polish*.

### Specialist AI: Task-Optimized Tools

*   **Perplexity's Superpower: Fast, Accurate Fetching.** It's fine-tuned for search, delivering specific facts (e.g., restaurant details, token limits) within seconds. Think of it as a replacement for Google's AI search mode, not a general reasoning chatbot.

*   **Notebook LM's Superpower: Source-Grounded Answers.** It only answers using documents you provide, minimizing hallucinations. It's perfect for verifying that drafts (like marketing materials or video scripts) don't contradict source information.

The core principle is **tool-task fit**. Most people only need a paid ChatGPT subscription, but power users can mix tools to leverage each one's unique strength.
