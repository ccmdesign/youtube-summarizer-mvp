## Summary

### Replit Revolutionizes Mobile App Creation
Replit has launched a groundbreaking feature that allows developers to **vibe code native mobile applications and publish them directly to app stores** with minimal clicks. This addresses key barriers like App Store configuration, payments, and security audits that previously kept vibe-coded projects primarily on the web. Early beta testers report the process is transformative, enabling app creation from a phone at a coffee shop and resulting in "10 out of 10 quality" applications.

### AI Funding and Adoption Milestones

*   **Replit's valuation soars:** Concurrent with this launch, Bloomberg reports Replit is closing a $400M funding round at a **$9 billion valuation**.

*   **Higsfield's hypergrowth:** The generative video platform Higsfield closed an $80M extension, hitting a **$1.3B valuation**. The 9-month-old startup boasts 15M users and has doubled its Annual Recurring Rate (ARR) from $100M to $200M in just two months, making it the fastest startup to reach $200M ARR—outpacing OpenAI, Slack, and Zoom. A significant shift is that **85% of its usage now comes from social media managers**, indicating a move beyond casual use to professional production workflows.

### Industry Shifts: Talent and Geopolitics

*   **Thinking Machines Labs' exodus:** The startup is experiencing a major talent drain, with three of its six co-founders leaving in recent months—several returning to OpenAI. Reports suggest more departures are imminent following internal misalignments on the company's product and technology direction.

*   **The US-China AI gap narrows:** Google Deep

Mind CEO Demis Hassabis stated that Chinese AI models are now **only "a matter of months behind"** their US counterparts, a dramatic reduction from a year or two ago. Models like ZAI, Kimmy K2, and Quen 3 are in the same ballpark, and China leads in some areas like video generation. However, Hassabis notes China has yet to prove it can make **truly novel breakthroughs**, like inventing a new architecture beyond the transformer. This is underscored by ZAI training a model entirely on Huawei's chip-and-software stack, proving China's developing a fully capable, independent AI ecosystem.
